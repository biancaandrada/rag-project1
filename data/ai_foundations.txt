Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a broad field of computer science focused on building systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include reasoning, learning from data, recognizing patterns, understanding language, and making decisions. Historically, AI began with symbolic approaches, where systems relied on rules and logic explicitly defined by humans. However, modern AI is dominated by machine learning, a paradigm where models learn patterns directly from data instead of relying on hardcoded rules.

Machine learning can be divided into supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning. In supervised learning, models are trained on labeled datasets, meaning each input has a corresponding correct output. In unsupervised learning, the model attempts to discover patterns in data without labels, often used for clustering or dimensionality reduction. Reinforcement learning involves agents interacting with environments and learning optimal strategies through rewards and penalties.

Deep learning, a subset of machine learning, uses neural networks with multiple layers to model complex patterns. These models have been especially successful in domains such as computer vision and natural language processing. Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT are trained on massive text corpora and can generate coherent, context-aware responses.

Despite the progress, AI systems still face challenges such as bias in data, lack of interpretability, and high computational costs. Ethical concerns are also central, including fairness, privacy, and misuse. Understanding the foundations of AI is essential for building systems that are both effective and responsible in real-world applications.

Embeddings are numerical representations of data, typically in the form of vectors, that capture semantic meaning. In natural language processing, embeddings map words, sentences, or documents into a high-dimensional vector space where similar meanings are located closer together.

Modern embeddings are generated using neural networks trained on large datasets. Models such as Word2Vec, GloVe, and more recently transformer-based embeddings, learn relationships between words based on their context. For example, the words “king” and “queen” would be closer in vector space than “king” and “banana.”

One of the key advantages of embeddings is that they allow for semantic search rather than keyword matching. Instead of looking for exact word matches, systems can find content that is conceptually similar. This is critical in applications like recommendation systems, clustering, and retrieval-based AI systems.

Embeddings are also used in tasks such as anomaly detection and classification. By converting data into vectors, machine learning models can operate more effectively on complex inputs like text or images. The choice of embedding model can significantly impact performance, as different models capture different types of relationships.

Despite their usefulness, embeddings have limitations. They can encode biases present in training data and may struggle with highly domain-specific language. Proper evaluation and selection of embedding models are therefore essential when building production systems.

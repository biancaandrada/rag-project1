Monaco is a small but highly developed city-state located between France and Italy on the French Riviera. It is known for luxury, wealth, and iconic landmarks such as the Monte Carlo Casino and the Formula 1 Grand Prix.

Despite its size, Monaco offers several key attractions. The Prince’s Palace (Palais Princier) is located in Monaco-Ville, the old town, and overlooks the Mediterranean. Visitors can witness the changing of the guard and explore historic streets.

The Monte Carlo district is the most famous part of Monaco. It features luxury hotels, high-end boutiques, and the Casino de Monte-Carlo. Even if you do not gamble, the architecture and atmosphere make it worth visiting.

The Oceanographic Museum is another major highlight. Built on a cliff overlooking the sea, it combines marine science with historical exhibits and is considered one of the best aquariums in Europe.

Monaco is extremely clean, safe, and well-organized. However, it is expensive, especially in terms of food and accommodation. Many visitors choose to stay in Nice and take a day trip.

Transportation to Monaco is straightforward. The TER train from Nice takes around 20 minutes and runs frequently. The journey itself is scenic, passing along the coastline. Buses are also available but slower.

Due to its compact size, Monaco can be explored mostly on foot. Elevators and escalators throughout the city help navigate its steep terrain.

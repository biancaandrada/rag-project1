Nice is the largest city on the French Riviera (Côte d’Azur) and serves as a central hub for travelers exploring the region. It combines Mediterranean beaches, Italian-influenced architecture, and a strong food culture rooted in local traditions.

The historic center, known as Vieux Nice (Old Town), is a dense network of narrow streets filled with colorful buildings, small shops, and traditional restaurants. The Cours Saleya market is a key attraction, offering fresh produce, flowers, spices, and local specialties. It is one of the best places to experience authentic Niçoise culture.

The Promenade des Anglais is the city’s most iconic seafront boulevard, stretching along the Mediterranean coastline. It is ideal for walking, cycling, and observing daily life in Nice. Castle Hill (Colline du Château) provides panoramic views over the city and is a must-visit for first-time visitors.

Nice is also known for its cuisine. Traditional dishes include socca (a chickpea pancake), pissaladière (a savory onion tart with anchovies), and salade niçoise. These dishes reflect the city’s Mediterranean identity and use simple, high-quality ingredients.

Transportation in Nice is efficient and well-organized. The city has a modern tram system connecting the airport to the city center and extending to key neighborhoods. Buses and regional trains (TER) provide easy access to nearby destinations such as Monaco, Cannes, and Antibes.

Nice is not just a tourist destination but also a strategic base for exploring the entire French Riviera due to its connectivity, infrastructure, and cultural diversity.

Public transportation on the French Riviera is efficient, affordable, and well-connected, making it possible to travel between Nice, Monaco, Cannes, and other towns without a car.

The TER (Train Express Régional) is the most important mode of transport for intercity travel. It runs frequently along the الساح coast and connects major destinations. Travel times are short: Nice to Monaco takes around 20 minutes, while Nice to Cannes takes about 30 minutes.

Tickets can be purchased at train stations, ticket machines, or online. It is important to validate tickets before boarding when required.

In Nice, the tram system is modern and connects the airport directly to the city center. This is the cheapest and most efficient way to travel from Nice Airport.

Buses are also available and often cheaper than trains, but they are slower due to traffic and multiple stops. However, they can reach locations not served by trains.

Monaco is accessible by both train and bus, but the train is generally preferred for speed and reliability. Cannes is also easily accessible by train and is part of the same coastal line.

For travelers planning multiple trips, day passes or regional passes may offer better value. Overall, public transport is the best option for exploring the French Riviera efficiently without dealing with parking or high car rental costs.
